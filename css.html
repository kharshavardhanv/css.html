Iam h1

Discard your ego, Do everything for Shiva’s sake. Do Everything with love and compassion. Help poor and needy people. Control your lust, anger and greed. (You can easily overcome these bad qualities by the grace of Shiva) Worship him with all faith and devotion. Time doesn’t matter. Worship him day and night. Remember, He is not anywhere if he is not in your heart. Shiva is There, Where the holy heart and pure mind is. Where love and compassion is. If you commit any sin unintentionally, Then kindly say Sorry to Shiva and do not repeat that mistake again.
Finally, The highest form of bhakti is to meditate on Shiva while doing any activity.Meditate on Shiva while walking, talking, reading or doing any activity. (This is indeed possible when you truly worship him since many years).

Mahashivaratri

Mahāśivārātri is a festival that celebrates the greatness of Lord Shiva. The festival is observed every year in the month of Śīlī, and it is one of the most important Hindu festivals. The festival commemorates the battle between the god Vishnu and the demon Mahāśiva. Mahāśiva was able to defeat Vishnu, and as a result, he was given supreme authority over the universe. The festival is celebrated with prayers and sacrifices to Lord Shiva, and it is a time for people to come together and celebrate their faith.

Greatness of Mahashivratri

Mahāśivārātri is celebrated every year on the day of the full moon in the month of Śivāda. The day is also known as Śivāda Pūjā and is a national holiday in India. On this day, people visit temples and offer prayers to Lord Shiva. The main event of Mahāśivārātri is the immersion of the linga (phallus) of Lord Shiva in the river Ganges.
